\textbf{NCERT class 12 Chapter 13(Probability)}\\
\textbf{Exercise 13.2 Problem 2}\\
Two cards are drawn at random and without replacement from a pack of 52 cards. Find the probability that both the cards are black.\\
\solution\\
Let's denote the random variable $X_1$ map to the set $\{ 0,1 \}$ where $X_1 = 0$ denote that the first card drawn is red and $X_1 = 1$ denote that the first card drawn is black.\\
Let's denote the random variable $X_2$ map to the set $\{ 0,1 \}$ where $X_2 = 0$ denote that the second card drawn is red and $X_2 = 1$ denote that the second card drawn is black.\\
The required probability is $\pr{X_1 = 1, X_2 = 1}$. Various Probability values are given in the table \eqref{table:table1}
\begin{table}[ht!]
    \input{Table1}
    \caption{}
	\label{table:table1}
\end{table}

We know that, by multiplication rule,
\begin{multline}
\pr{X_2=1,X_1=1} = \pr{X_1=1} \times 
\\
\pr{X_2=1|X_1=1}
\end{multline}
\begin{align}
    \implies \pr{X_2=1,X_1=1} &= \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{25}{51}\\
    \implies \pr{X_2=1,X_1=1} &= \frac{25}{102}
\end{align}
