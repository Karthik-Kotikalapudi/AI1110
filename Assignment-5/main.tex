\textbf{NCERT class 11}\\
\textbf{Exercise 16.2 Q1}\\
A die is rolled. Let $E$ be the event "die shows 4" and $F$ be the event "die shows even number". Are $E$ and $F$ mutually exclusive?\\
\solution
Let's denote the solution of the outcome by a random variable $X\in \cbrak{1,2,3,4,5,6}$, where for each $X = i, i$ denotes the number shown on the die. Therefore, the events $E$ and $F$ are
\begin{align}
    E &= \cbrak{X = 4}
    \\
    F &= \cbrak{X = 2 \cup X = 4 \cup X = 6}
\end{align}
Two events $A$ and $B$ are said to be mutually exclusive if $A \cap B = \phi$.\\
Here $E \cap F = \cbrak{X = 4} \neq \phi$\\
$\therefore E$ and $F$ are not mutually exclusive events.
